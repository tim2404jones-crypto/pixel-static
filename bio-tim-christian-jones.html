Bio — Tim Christian Jones

I’ve always felt it’s important to know the person who’ll be alongside you on your wedding day — quietly present, observing, and capturing what truly matters as it unfolds.

I’m Tim Christian Jones, a wedding photographer based in Leeds. I photograph weddings across Yorkshire and beyond, drawn to days that feel personal, relaxed, and full of real connection.

I don’t place much value on titles or claims. Wedding photography isn’t something I measure through awards or lists — it’s measured in trust, consistency, and the quiet moments couples recognise when they see their photographs again.

How I Found My Way Into Wedding Photography

Photography has been part of my life for as long as I can remember. I studied at Leeds Beckett University, graduating with a BA (Hons), but my connection to photography began much earlier — shaped by film, by grain, and by images that felt honest rather than perfect.

Before photographing weddings full time, I worked in retail and spent a summer travelling through Brazil with my camera. Photographing people there — and seeing their reactions when they saw themselves in the images — changed everything. Photography stopped being something I enjoyed, and became something that felt quietly important.

That feeling has never left. I’m drawn to the in-between moments — the glances, the laughter, the pauses — the parts of the day that often matter most when you look back.

I feel genuinely lucky to do this. Photographing weddings never feels like a performance or a production. Just a chance to document people as they are, on a day that means everything to them.

Life Outside the Camera

Outside of weddings, I’m someone who values calm and balance. I’m a healthy eater, a lover of nature, and happiest around animals — they have a way of reminding you to slow down and be present.

Music has always been a constant, especially the kind that feels tied to a time and a feeling. From Shocking Blue through to Take That and the Backstreet Boys, it’s less about genre and more about the memories it carries.

I spend a lot of time walking locally — places like Kirkstall Abbey and Golden Acre Park are regular favourites — and when I find myself by the coast, a stop at the Magpie Café in Whitby is hard to resist. Simple pleasures, done well.
